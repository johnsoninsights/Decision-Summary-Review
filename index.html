Decision Review Analysis

Three points
to consider

The cost is lower than the original estimate suggests

Hosting 10–15 client sites on a modern platform like Vercel runs approximately $40–200 per month total — not per site. That's competitive with, or better than, our current per-site hosting cost, even during busy enrollment periods.

  • Billing is based on team size and actual usage — not the number of client sites hosted. More sites don't automatically mean a bigger bill.
  • Benefits microsites are among the lightest possible workloads — mostly text and static pages. PDF documents, which drive the most data, can be stored separately at near-zero cost using widely available cloud storage.
  • The original cost concerns in the prior analysis were valid questions — but when modeled against our actual traffic profile and site type, the numbers are well within budget at scale.

AI tools make code-based workflows dramatically faster

The build-time estimates in the prior analysis assume a developer working manually. AI coding tools — built directly into the code editor — change the equation significantly. This advantage applies to code, not to drag-and-drop page builders.

  • Tasks that once took hours — building page layouts, connecting content systems, writing repetitive component code — now take minutes when an AI agent understands your codebase and can generate from a spec.
  • WordPress and Elementor's value is the visual editor — which AI cannot accelerate. The gap between the two workflows is actively widening in favor of code-first development as AI tooling matures.
  • The original estimate of 8–11 days to build a site was written without factoring in AI-assisted development. In practice, with the right setup in place, that timeline compresses significantly.

Premium design is a business asset, not a luxury

Most important

A design system built once — 4 to 5 polished templates — gives us fast turnaround on every site that follows, while delivering a product that looks and feels premium. This isn't just about aesthetics.

  • Clients don't only evaluate whether their employees found their benefits information. They evaluate whether the product reflects on them — whether it's something they're proud to put in front of their workforce.
  • A site that looks and feels premium signals that the benefit communication was taken seriously. That perception drives client satisfaction, retention, and referrals — it's a business outcome, not a design preference.
  • A reusable design system means we don't sacrifice speed for quality. After the initial investment, new client sites are built from proven templates — same fast turnaround, better end product every time.

The gap isn't that Next.js sites look a little nicer. It's that fluid animations, smooth transitions, and modern interactions come naturally in code — and feel bolted-on in a page builder. For a product people interact with during one of the most important decisions of their year, that difference matters.
